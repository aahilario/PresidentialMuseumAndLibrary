PREFACE 

This Catalog is a record of all the books in 
the library, arranged alphabetically by the authors' 
names* Each book is listed by giving in order its 
author, title, and place and date of publication, 
followed by its Call number. 

The Call nujnber consists of the class and author 
marks, which have been assigned to every book. It 
corresponds to the notation indicated on its cover, 
and shows its location on the shelf. 

All the books are arranged on the shelves in 
simple numerical order according to their call nimibers. 
Thus a book falling under Class 512 (Algebra) precedes 
Class 513 (Geometry), but follows Class 511 (Arith- 
metic). This brings all books on the same or kindred 
subjects together. 

According to the Classification scheme used, 

the books in the library are grouped into ten main 

classes v/ith notations as follows: 

000-099 GENERAL WORKS: Books that deal with 
no particular subject, such as en- 
cyclopedias, dictionaries, period- 
icals, nev/spapers, etc. 



iii 



100-199 PHILOSOPHY: Psychology, ethics, 
etc. 

200-299 RELIGION: Christian and non- 
Christian beliefs. 

300-399 SOCIOLOGY: Government, economics, 
law, education, etc. 

400-499 LANGUAGE: Grammars, dictionaries, , 
etc. in all languages. 

500-599 SCIENCE: Mathematics, astronomy, 
physics, chemistry, geology, 
"biology, botany, zoology. 

600-699 USEFUL ARTS: Medicine, engineering, 
agriculture, home economics, etc. 

700-799 FINE ARTS: Architecture, sculpture, 
painting, engraving, music, etc. 

800-899 LITERATURE: Poems, dramas, essays, 
etc. in all languages. 

900-999 HISTORY: Travel, collective bio- 
graphy (giving the lives of several 
persons) histories of all countries. 

TRAVEL: Includes description in all 
countries and has the number 910-919. 

BIOGRAPHY: For collective 920 is 
used; for individual 921, under 
which the "lives" are arranged al- 
phabetically by the name of the 
person v/ritten about. 

FICTIONS. No classification has been prov_ 
ided for fictions in English; but 
these are arranged alphabetically 
by the author's surnames. Fictions 
in other languages bear their cor- 
responding class nvunbers.
